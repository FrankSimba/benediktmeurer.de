I am Benedikt Meurer, a software developer and research associate living in Siegen, a medium-sized town in the south Westphalian part of North Rhine-Westphalia, about 100 kilometers from Cologne. I received my diploma in applied computer science with electrical engineering from the Universität Siegen in 2007, and since then I have been working as a research associate at the Lehrstuhl für Compilerbau und Softwareanalyse (and the Lehrstuhl für Mikrosystementwurf in 2007/2008).

Besides that, I have been working as a software developer for os-cillation GmbH since 2003, where the major part of my work is spent on KVM-over-IP solutions with Embedded Linux, and some iOS related development, but I also worked on the the Xfld Linux Live CD, the Xfce installers, Terminal and other random projects.

I spent a rather large amount of my spare time on developing and contributing to open source projects. During my studies from 2002 to 2007, I developed and maintained various core parts of the Xfce desktop environment, including the new file manager Thunar, which I wrote from scratch to replace the unpopular Xffm file manager, the Session Manager, the Xfce Libraries, the Terminal, innumerable plugins and goodies, and several other core components.

My GnuPG key

Encrypted mail preferred. I sign most of my private emails with the GNU Privacy Guard, an open source implementation of OpenPGP. If you want to contact me by email, you can download my public key here or from the PGP keyservers. To retrieve my public key from a PGP keyserver and import it into your public key ring, open a shell and type the following command:

{% highlight console %} $ gpg --keyserver wwwkeys.pgp.net --recv-key FD5E0F0F {% endhighlight %}

For additional information, you should consult the manual shipped with your GnuPG distribution and visit the The GNU Privacy Guard Documentation, where you can find the GNU Privacy Guard Handbook, which should be the first book to read, in order to get started with GnuPG.
